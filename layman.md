FraudLens is a command-line tool for testing fraud detection rules against a history of transactions. You give it a CSV file of past payments — each labeled as legitimate or fraudulent — and it runs those payments through a set of rules (like "flag large amounts" or "flag transactions in foreign countries") to show you how well the rules would have caught real fraud. The output tells you things like precision (how often an alert was a real fraud) and recall (what fraction of real fraud was caught), so you can tune your rules with confidence before using them in production. It is aimed at payment security teams, fintech developers, and compliance engineers who need a reproducible, scriptable harness they can drop into CI pipelines.
